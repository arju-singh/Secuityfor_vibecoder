        Questions

        Good to know.

        The things people ask before their first scan.

        Is it safe to run against a live site?

        Yes. SentryScan is strictly non-intrusive — it loads pages the way a browser does and probes safely, but never attempts an exploit or any destructive action. Scans of localhost and private networks are blocked outright.

        Do I need to install anything on the target?

        No agents and nothing to install. You just provide a URL, an API endpoint, or a zip of your source. The render test uses a real headless Chromium on the SentryScan server, not on your target.

        Where do my results and credentials go?

        Nothing is stored server-side. Scan history lives only in your own browser, and any auth headers you supply are used for that single scan and never persisted. The only outbound call is to the public OSV.dev database to check dependency versions.

        How is the score calculated?

        Each suite contributes weighted findings by severity (critical → info). The result is a 0–100 grade with a per-category breakdown, so you can see exactly which area is dragging the score and what to fix first.

        Can I test something that needs a login?

        Yes — open the “Authenticated scan” box and add headers like Authorization: Bearer … or a session Cookie, and the scanner will test pages as that logged-in user. Save reusable header sets as named profiles. Only use credentials you own.

        What can I export or share?

        Every report exports to JSON for tooling or to a clean, print-styled PDF for hand-off. Findings include evidence, a copy-paste repro, and a concrete remediation for each issue.
